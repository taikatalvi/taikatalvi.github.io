This project presents a fully functional infrared scanning system designed for real-time thermal imaging applications. The system combines precision optics, embedded control, and digital signal processing to create detailed thermal maps of observed scenes.

The optical core consists of a 48mm diameter spherical mirror that focuses infrared radiation onto a single-element photodetector positioned precisely in the focal plane. This configuration provides excellent thermal sensitivity while maintaining a compact form factor.

The mechanical scanning mechanism employs a servo motor that rotates the entire optical assembly through 180 degrees, enabling coverage of the target area. A custom-designed limiting plate works in conjunction with an optical interrupter to provide precise timing control, ensuring that temperature readings are captured only during specific scanning positions. This approach allows the system to perform line-by-line spatial scanning, building up a complete thermal image over time.

At the heart of the electronic control system lies an Atmel ATmega16 microcontroller, which orchestrates the entire scanning process. The microcontroller generates PWM signals to drive the servo motor while simultaneously monitoring the optical interrupter for timing cues. When the limiting plate triggers the optical sensor, the system begins capturing temperature data from the IR photodetector and transmits this information to a connected PC via UART interface.

The digital processing chain handles the conversion from analog thermal signals to meaningful visual data. Real-time interpolation techniques reconstruct the spatial thermal distribution, with each pixel's color corresponding to the measured temperature at that location.

The complete system demonstrates successful thermal imaging capabilities, validated through extensive experimental testing that confirms both the optical performance and the accuracy of the digital signal processing algorithms.

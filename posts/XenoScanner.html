XenoScanner is an iOS app published on the App Store that turns an iPhone into a retro-futuristic sensor console — a glowing phosphor-green CRT terminal in the aesthetic of classic sci-fi, with two real scanning modes driven by actual sensor data.

Motion Tracker is the iconic circular sonar scope with a rotating sweep, range rings and blips. Contacts are not fake: they come from LiDAR depth-map motion detection fused with ARKit body anchors, positioned by direction and distance. A synthesized ping rises in pitch as a contact gets closer, and a world-anchored compass keeps the radar oriented as you turn.

Structure Scan draws the live LiDAR mesh of your surroundings as a glowing green wireframe — fresnel edges plus a sweeping scan plane rendered over the camera feed with custom Metal shaders, like a starship deck schematic.

Both modes are unified by a CRT post-process: scanlines, vignette, a rolling refresh band and a procedural boot sequence. On LiDAR-equipped iPhones the scanner fuses depth data with camera motion for accurate contacts; on other devices it falls back to a camera-based motion estimate.

Everything runs 100% on-device — no accounts, no network, no analytics, no data collection. Built with Swift, SwiftUI, ARKit and Metal. Get it on the App Store ↗
